# critical-thinking

`critical-thinking` is an interactive critical-thinking skill for examining a user's own view, a claim, an argument, a decision, or an observed phenomenon. It helps make the reasoning visible, evaluate evidence, uncover hidden assumptions, test bias and rhetoric, and produce a calibrated judgment.

Use it when the user has analyzable content and wants to know whether a conclusion is well supported.

## Best For

- Evaluating a personal opinion before acting on it.
- Testing a claim, public statement, recommendation, or argument.
- Distinguishing facts from interpretations, values, predictions, and recommendations.
- Checking whether reasons and evidence actually support a conclusion.
- Finding hidden assumptions, ambiguous terms, weak evidence, and missing alternatives.
- Surfacing cognitive bias, rhetorical pressure, or reasoning fallacies.
- Producing a more precise, lower-risk, better-calibrated conclusion.

## Workflow

1. **Intake**: collect the user's view, phenomenon, argument, decision, or text plus background.
2. **Main claim**: clarify the central conclusion and its scope.
3. **Fact/opinion split**: separate factual claims, interpretations, value judgments, predictions, and recommendations.
4. **Argument map**: identify reasons, evidence, and implied support.
5. **Evidence test**: evaluate relevance, sufficiency, credibility, representativeness, precision, and alternatives.
6. **Assumptions and ambiguity**: surface load-bearing assumptions and unclear terms.
7. **Bias and fallacy check**: identify likely reasoning distortions without turning labels into accusations.
8. **Strong opposition**: steelman objections, contrary evidence, and alternative explanations.
9. **Calibrated judgment**: accept, reject, suspend, narrow, or revise the claim with confidence level.
10. **Next evidence**: identify what to verify, ask, measure, or observe next.

## Example Invocation

```text
Use $critical-thinking to analyze my view: remote work always lowers team collaboration quality. Background: our team has had several delayed projects since switching to hybrid work.
```

Chinese is also fine:

```text
使用 $critical-thinking 帮我分析这个看法：我觉得现在年轻人不愿意吃苦，所以工作稳定性变差了。背景是……
```

## Notes

This skill requires the user to start with a concrete view, claim, phenomenon, decision, argument, or text. It should not begin with a generic lecture about critical thinking.
