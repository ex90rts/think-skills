# Methodology: 5So Consequence-Chain Thinking

5So means repeatedly asking "so what?" or "so then what?" about a phenomenon. It is a logic-chain inference process for exploring what a phenomenon may ultimately lead to.

## Core Difference From 5Why

- **5Why** asks backward toward causes: "Why did this happen?"
- **5So** asks forward toward consequences: "So what will this lead to?"

Do not mix the two in the main chain. If causes are unclear, ask only enough to understand the starting phenomenon, then return to consequence inference.

## Layer Model

5So commonly moves through these layers:

- **Surface conclusion**: the most visible or immediate result.
- **Shallow result**: the first practical consequence.
- **Deep result**: second-order effects that emerge after the first result changes behavior, incentives, resources, or relationships.
- **Deeper result**: structural, systemic, identity, strategic, or irreversible consequences.
- **Root or terminal result**: the endpoint where the chain is either actionable, low-probability, or no longer useful to extend.

The labels are guides, not rigid stages.

## Step Template

For each "so what" step, capture:

- `claim`: what may happen next.
- `mechanism`: why the previous step makes this more likely.
- `assumptions`: what must be true for this step to occur.
- `probability`: high, medium, or low.
- `impact`: high, medium, or low.
- `usefulness`: whether this step gives practical guidance.

## Stopping Rule

The method is called 5So, but the user should not mechanically ask exactly five times. Stop when:

- Probability becomes too low to guide real action.
- The next step is mostly imagination.
- The current layer is already actionable.
- The chain reaches a meaningful endpoint.
- The inference starts repeating itself.
- The time horizon is no longer relevant.

When the chain stops at a low-probability endpoint, wait for time and evidence to move the earlier layers forward. Later layers may become more likely after earlier consequences appear.

## Useful Prompts

Starting:

- What phenomenon are we examining?
- If this keeps happening, what changes first?
- Who is affected first?
- Which resource, incentive, belief, or behavior changes first?

Continuing:

- So what does that make more likely?
- If that happens, what does it change next?
- What second-order effect follows?
- Who reacts to this, and how?
- What new constraint appears?
- What new opportunity or risk appears?
- What becomes harder to reverse?

Branching:

- What uncertainty determines the next layer?
- If the response is strong, what happens?
- If nobody reacts, what happens?
- What is the base path?
- What is the adverse path?

Stopping:

- Is the next step still useful, or only dramatic?
- Has probability dropped below practical value?
- What evidence would make this next layer worth revisiting?
- What should we monitor before extending the chain?

## Quality Bar

A strong 5So chain:

- Starts from a concrete phenomenon.
- Moves one consequence at a time.
- Shows the mechanism between steps.
- Keeps uncertainty visible.
- Separates near-term likely effects from distant speculative outcomes.
- Ends with watchpoints and action implications.

A weak 5So chain:

- Jumps from a small event to a dramatic endpoint.
- Repeats the same consequence in different words.
- Hides assumptions.
- Treats a possible future as certain.
- Keeps asking "so what" after the chain has stopped producing useful guidance.
